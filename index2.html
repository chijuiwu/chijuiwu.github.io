Paper: EagleSense: Tracking People and Devices in Interactive Spaces using Real-Time Top-View Depth-Sensing

My name is Chi-Jui Wu, and I am also known as Charles. I have recently graduated from University College London (UK) with a Master of Research in Computational Statistics and Machine Learning. With the support of my awesome supervisors and many sleepless nights, I am very honoured to have my Master's thesis work accepted to CHI, a very prestigious international conference in Human Computer Interaction (HCI) research (~24% acceptance rate).

HCI is about studying, designing, building, and evaluating interactions between computers/systems and human agents. HCI research empowers individuals and groups to become more efficient in their everyday task and to interact with technologies in novel and natural ways. This includes developing natural interfaces between us and the ubiquitous, heterogeneous multi-device ecology. EagleSense is a novel non-invasive tracking system for making sense of an interactive space through a bird's eye-view depth camera. It recognizes, in real time, people's position, orientation, and activity, including their use of mobile devices (See video). EagleSense can support new cross-device applications as a part of small-group activities to leverage the tracking information of surrounding people and devices. It will also be employed in my future research for the design of novel cross-device interactions.

About Chi-Jui

I am a Human-Computer Interaction researcher. My research interests include sensing technologies, mobile devices, ubiquitous computing, proxemic interaction, and machine learning. I build novel supporting toolkits for group collaborations and multi-device interactions, and leverage machine intelligence to enhance existing sensing capabilities within interactive spaces. Recently, I have developed a new tracking infrastructure that enables HCI researchers to build and evaluate proxemic-aware, activity and device-centric interfaces. EagleSense is a system infrastructure for tracking people and devices in interactive spaces using real-time top-view depth-sensing. It enables HCI researchers to build new proxemic and multi-device interactions. Our results show that the system can recognize six different human postures and activities with 90% accuracy at 30 FPS.

Out of Sight is a toolkit that extends the Kinect v2 SDK for tracking occluded human joint positions. It combines the skeleton stream of multiple Kinects using a coordinate transformation algorithm to track the position of people during occlusion. It enables applications to "see through" obstructions. The tracking accuracy is within 16cm.

Recomputation is the process of creating a working environment for a piece of computer program, so that it can be referenced, maintained, and deployed in the future. Recompute is a web application which automates the process of recomputation. It takes a GitHub repository URL as input and creates a virtual machine, within which the program is rebuilt from source. Anyone can download the virtual machine and run the program from anywhere.
